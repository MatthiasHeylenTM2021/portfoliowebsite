Internship - Number Porting

During my 13-week internship at Telenet, I had the opportunity to dive deep into various aspects of telecom and software development, gaining hands-on experience in a professional environment. One of the key areas I focused on was understanding the basics of telecommunications, which provided me with a solid foundation in how networks and services operate. Alongside this, I got introduced to AWS, learning about cloud services and how to effectively use AWS tools for deployment and scaling.

A significant part of my internship involved working on automation testing. I learned how to create automated tests using Cucumber, which helped me improve the efficiency of testing and made the process much more streamlined. I also became familiar with tools like SonarQube, where I worked on analyzing code coverage, removing bugs, and cleaning up code to ensure better maintainability. This experience gave me a clearer understanding of the importance of maintaining high-quality code, something I’ve been able to carry forward into my learning.

In addition to technical skills, I developed my knowledge in working with different web frameworks. I had the chance to explore React and Vue, both of which gave me a broader perspective on front-end development and the importance of building responsive and scalable applications. These frameworks introduced me to new ways of thinking about user interfaces and the value of component-based development.

Collaboration and communication were also key aspects of my internship. I was involved in presenting my work to colleagues, giving me the chance to develop my presentation skills and clearly articulate technical concepts to others. The team operated using agile methodologies, with sprints lasting two weeks, regular retrospectives after each sprint, and weekly reviews. I participated actively in these agile rituals, which helped me understand how iterative development works in practice and how teams continuously improve their processes. Additionally, daily stand-ups kept the team aligned and focused on our objectives.

Data Visualisation Dashboard

For this project, I used Qlik Sense to create a comprehensive dashboard focused on addressing the climate crisis by exploring three Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs): Goal 13 (Climate Action), Goal 14 (Life Below Water), and Goal 15 (Life on Land). The purpose of the dashboard was to provide meaningful insights tailored to a specific audience, utilizing storytelling capabilities to effectively communicate the progress and opportunities related to these goals. The dashboard was designed to be visually engaging and technically robust, leveraging multiple visualization techniques.

I also incorporated images and visual storytelling elements to enhance the user experience and make the dashboard more engaging. This included clear labels, intuitive navigation, and strategically placed graphics to guide users through the narrative. The design was developed entirely within the constraints of Qlik Sense, adhering to the project requirements of not using additional plugins or datasets.

The final dashboard not only met the technical requirements but also provided a powerful tool for understanding the interplay between climate action, ocean health, and land preservation, supporting informed decision-making for the audience.

Project 4.0

The Visi-Age Project aimed to support independent elderly living by monitoring movement and detecting falls using computer vision technology for a tech company based in Belgium called Mediaan. Me and an another application development student worked together for this project with an AI-team and Cloud-team. My primary responsibilities included designing and developing both the front-end and back-end systems, ensuring a cohesive and functional user experience. The application alerts caregivers to falls and provides daily movement data, empowering them to respond effectively to patients' needs.

For the fall detection system, the AI-team developed a method to track keypoints of the body and calculate the nose's vertical position relative to a dynamically adjusted threshold line. This line adapts to the person's distance from the camera, compensating for perspective changes in 2D video footage. If the person crosses this threshold at a certain velocity or disappears suddenly, a fall is detected. The videos are sent to Azure Blob Storage, while alerts are triggered and sent to the back-end for caregiver notification.

On the back-end, I built a robust API using Java/Spring Boot, configuring the database for secure and efficient data storage. My implementation included features for user management, including roles for dependents, confidants, and caregivers. I also developed endpoints for handling fall alerts, storing movement data, and linking videos from Azure. By setting up Swagger for API documentation, I ensured that the system was easily accessible and maintainable.

The front-end, created with Angular, serves as the caregiver's interface, displaying alerts and movement data on an intuitive dashboard. My work included creating reusable components, setting up routing for seamless navigation, and configuring environments for production and development.

Orientation Project

During our orientation project, our team worked on creating a static website for the Vondelingenluik, an initiative aimed at providing a safe and discreet solution for abandoning babies. The project involved not only building the website but also designing and branding our own mock company, which we named OneWeb. This exercise allowed us to develop a professional online presence for the Vondelingenluik, combining functional and emotional elements to effectively communicate its mission.

My primary responsibility was providing translations and developing the Werking page, which explains how the Vondelingenluik operates and provides step-by-step instructions on safely delivering a baby. I carefully structured the page to ensure clarity and accessibility for a diverse audience, balancing technical accuracy with empathetic language. By utilizing user-friendly design principles, I ensured that critical information was easy to locate and understand, reinforcing the sense of safety and support central to the initiative.

The development process involved using HTML, CSS, and a focus on responsive design to ensure the page was accessible across devices. I incorporated visual aids, including infographics, to illustrate the operation of the Vondelingenluik, enhancing comprehension for users with varying levels of technical literacy. This attention to detail ensured that the page not only met functional requirements but also aligned with the emotional sensitivity of the subject matter.
